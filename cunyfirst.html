Summary

Mission

CUNYfirst is the backbone of student administration for the City University of New York. Following a major system-wide redesign, the platform experienced significant usability friction, resulting in widespread student inability to successfully enroll in courses. As a Technical Specialist at RFCUNY, my mission was to conduct an emergency UX research initiative to diagnose navigation failures, advocate for student needs, and propose data-driven design iterations to restore a functional enrollment funnel.

My Contributions

I spearheaded the research phase by performing Heuristic Evaluations of the new enrollment interface to identify violations of usability standards. I conducted User interviews and surveys with a diverse student body to map out the "Redesign Friction Points." Using these insights, I created a series of User Pain Point Maps that highlighted specific bottlenecks in the class-search-to-checkout flow. I then translated these findings into a set of Hifi Wireframes and high-level technical recommendations, such as revert back to the class availability table instead of the new calendar view, which were presented to stakeholders to guide the next phase of system patches.
